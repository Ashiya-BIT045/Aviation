Pilot training courses

Pilot training courses focus on teaching flying skills and include licenses like PPL, CPL, and ATPL. These prepare individuals to fly aircraft for personal, commercial, or airline purposes. Additional certifications, such as instrument and multi-engine ratings, enhance expertise.

Aviation Management

Aviation management courses equip students with knowledge of airport operations, airline logistics, and safety regulations. They prepare individuals for roles like airport managers, airline operations managers, or aviation consultants. These courses blend business principles with aviation-specific training.

Aircraft Maintanance

Aircraft maintenance courses train individuals to inspect, repair, and maintain aircraft systems. Programs like Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME) focus on ensuring aircraft safety and efficiency. Graduates often work as maintenance engineers or avionics specialists.

Private Pilot License

This entry-level course teaches the basics of flying small aircraft for personal use. It covers flight maneuvers, navigation, and air regulations. Holders cannot fly for commercial purposes.

Commercial Pilot License

Designed for aspiring professional pilots, this course focuses on advanced flying skills. It allows individuals to work for airlines or charter companies. Practical flight hours and exams are required for certification.

Aircraft Maintanence Engineering

This program trains individuals to maintain, inspect, and repair aircraft systems. It includes both mechanical and avionics specializations. Graduates become certified aircraft maintenance engineers.
